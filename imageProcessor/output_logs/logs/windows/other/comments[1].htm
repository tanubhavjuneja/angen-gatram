  • Keith

    "Disappearing" can have terrible lingering consequences for a family. In the mid 1990s a successful husband and father of 3 children, disappeared.

    His family was native to NJ but moved to Az. when he was transferred for an executive postion within Hewlett-Packard. His family enjoyed a wealthy lifestyle; an upscale home in the Scottsdale foothills, his wife retired from computer programming to own a horse for recreational riding, and weekends were spent playing exclusive golf resorts with his son, etc.

    Yet, one week prior to his daughter’s wedding, he vanished. His Cadillac was found a month later in a hospital parking lot. He maxed-out multiple, high credit limit credit cards. He did not pay property taxes for many years. His wife had to file for bankruptcy and sell the house and horse. She later required a dual mastectomy and found herself unemployable at her previous capacity in IT - her life turned upside down and inside out. The children have had to write-off a father.

    It seems this story was covered with levity inappropriate to the innocent victims and the utterly unanswerable questions that will haunt them for their lifetimes.
